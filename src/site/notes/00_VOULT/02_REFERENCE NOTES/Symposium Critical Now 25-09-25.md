## Critical Now: Notes on Urgency and Performance

### Performance as Violence and Witness

- **The Drone Incident:** Red drones over Copenhagen airport—performative acts that function as a performance of violence and threat.
    
- **The Core Question:** What kind of utterance or expression should be used? What is the role of documentary theater, fact, and fiction?
    
- **Presence vs. Absence:** Navigating the stage between being there and the void.
    
- **The "Now Now Now":** The relentless progress of the present moment vs. tangible experience.
    
- **The Unimaginable:** We are witnessing the "now" of genocide; the unimaginable is becoming reality.

### Eleonora Fabião: Circumstances and Negotiation

- **The Context:** A world full of the visible and invisible, the living dead and the dead living.
    
- **2021 Project:** Mapping public institutions (education, health, legislation, culture) between heaven and earth.
    
- **The Chairs:** She negotiated to obtain one chair from every state in Brazil. A massive effort of institutional negotiation to secure 27 chairs representing each state.
    
- **Networking:** On November 27, she invited representatives for a networking meeting.
    
- **The Exchange:** She returned the chairs but swapped them—creating "galleries" where a hospital chair went to a theater, and so on.
    
- **The Ethics of Action:** To desire a bomb, then meticulously disarm it. An obstinacy in and for vitality.
    
- **The City:** What action generates the specific movement that makes the city the city I want to live in?

### Trama (Web/Plot) & Lia Rodrigues (2023–Ongoing)

The work explores four dimensions of labor:

1. **Task:** The physical work.
    
2. **Struggle:** The work of staying alive.
    
3. **Liberation:** Work as a social task, a right, and a form of freedom.
    
4. **Rituals:** Work as a sacred act.
    

- **Performance Pressure:** Using actions like the "Glass of Water" performance to create tension.
    
- **Framing:** When does a performance begin and end? It is unframable.

### VA-BENE: Life and Live Art Confrontations

- **Identity:** "sHit" if not "She."
    
- **Transformation:** Creating rituals that are dangerous and painful to spark change. She cut herself to "become a woman."
    
- **Strategy:** Understand the weapons used by the enemy and use them against them.
    
- **The Divine:** Measuring oneself against a God, against religion and the procession.
    
- **Legacy:** Dead people don't die. She used her home to create a residency, building a future community for new generations.
    
- **Privilege:** Using her own privilege to create what she never had.
    
- **The Brave Space:** A curatorial residency must have sense: for whom and why? Not a "safe space," but a **brave space** that is uncomfortable for everyone.

### Agency, Rest, and Negotiation

- **The Trap of Rest:** We cannot rest, but we must celebrate. Resting is individualistic, expensive, and staying in society is exhausting.
    
- **The Ocean Metaphor:** Agency depends on position. One person in the ocean falls and drowns, fighting the waves with no way to rest. Another person sailing the ocean has the luxury to rest.
    
- **Institutional Friction:** When two elephants fight, it is the ground that suffers.

### Forensic Architecture (Renata Cavallo)

- **Focus:** Areas affected by conflict and border regimes.
    
- **The Forensic Gaze:** Counter-forensics—examining the shadows because public institutions cannot be trusted.
    
- **Investigative Approach:** Using 3D modeling to examine hospital explosions in Gaza.
    
- **Performative Research:** Research itself is a performance.
    
- **Memory:** Memory has an architecture; 3D models help trace and reconstruct human memory.
    
- **The Law:** A lack of belief in the law as a tool to fix things.

### Rural Urgency: Laboratory for Aesthetics and Ecology

- **Slow Agriculture:** Moving away from industrial farming processes.
    
- **Pedagogy of the Land:** The threshing floor acts as a teacher.
    
- **Speculative Practice:** Speculation arises from "in-body" questions and labor—a focus on the material itself.
    
- **The Harvest School:** A moisture of empathy.
