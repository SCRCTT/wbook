The term "Molecular Revolution" (the title of one of Félix Guattari's essay collections, _Molecular Revolution and Class Struggle_) represents a key concept in his radical critique.

The molecular revolution is defined as inseparable from a revolution on a larger, more visible scale (molar). Guattari argues that future major social upheavals have become inseparable from a multitude of molecular revolutions within the economy of desire.

### Fundamental Characteristics of the Molecular Revolution

- **Relationship with Social Crisis:** The Molecular Revolution does not only concern political systems but leads to a questioning of all, even the smallest, cogs and mechanisms of society.
    
- **Aspiration to Desire and Freedom:** The Molecular Revolution implies a multitude of molecular mutations of desire that have been liberated. The "molecular" is characterized as the emergence of lines of flight that combine with the objective lines of de-territorialization of the system to create an irrepressible aspiration for new areas of freedom.
    
- **Transversality and Micro-Politics:** This process implies a gradual branching of the agents of the molecular revolution beneath various systems of power. Such agents challenge established logics and require a molecular analysis, which is the local expression of molecular forces that combine theory and action.
    
- **Transformation of Objectives:** Molecular revolutions are not solely centered on quantitative goals (such as standard of living) but question the quality of life and the "right to desire." They require reconsidering the entire purpose of work, leisure, family life, gender relations, and the perception of time.
    
- **Challenge to Traditional Systems:** These revolutions represent profound challenges to social systems that undermine traditional relations of production, traditional social and family systems, and traditional attitudes toward the body, sex, and the universe.
    

Guattari believes that the revolutionary movement, by working on small-scale "mini-projects" (such as neighborhood committees or daycare centers), can contribute to launching the Molecular Revolution, transforming the current situation into a turning point in history.
