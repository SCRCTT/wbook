# [Global Conceptualism Revisited - Journal #155](https://www.e-flux.com/journal/155/674132/global-conceptualism-revisited)

## Highlights

Different countries had their “’68” at different moments, some like Japan and Latin America actually dating to way before. That gave us the model for “local clocks” and released us from a single universal history of art and hegemonic timekeeping. In turn, Peter Wollen’s analysis of the differences between Sol LeWitt’s and Joseph Kosuth’s visions for conceptual art led us to differentiate between conceptual art as a style and conceptualism as a strategy. Both these points helped us focus on the idea of decentering ==the history of art into many histories that responded to local events and crises.==
  
Learning from advertising, the emphasis in these works moves from delivering declarations to creating opinions. Conceptualist photographic work in particular moved beyond straight documentation and focused on the creation of opinion through the combination of images. Urgency and the creation of militancy, I believe, have marked conceptualist strategies on the periphery and have made them something very different from hegemonic conceptual art. Conceptual art was already and will remain an aesthetic period, like cubism or Dadaism. Conceptualist strategies, however, represent an attitude, and this makes me believe that they will keep their relevance for a long time.
  
====The longevity of artworks is not based on how well they are manufactured or how they appeal to our taste, but on how interesting the problems are and what they unleash in our thinking. More importantly, conceptual works involve the viewer in a more active relation and, unexpectedly, people have to start thinking.== This should have consequences that transcend art and therefore make the works much more interesting. There is a more general pedagogic quality to this kind of work that I would like to discuss here and that has nothing to do with simplistic didactic approaches (which is not to say that such approaches were absent from the exhibition).
  

## Concept of credit in pedagogy

The US introduced the concept of credits—the ultimate educational symbol of quantitative pedagogy—around 1910. Since 1999, thanks to the Bologna Process, the credit system slowly became adopted in the rest of the world. The big irony is that credits were not created to improve education but to secure the retirement rights of US teachers in a measurable way.

Big data, organized by algorithms and filters in search engines, accommodates and absorbs errors rather than discarding them, and uses estimation and fuzziness to achieve more precision and predictive power than counting. In everyday life, infinity is now administered through patterns and connections.
