## The Design Process (Munari Method Example)

### Problem:
Highlight the problem that needs to be solved; **Making a green rice dish.**

### Problem Definition:
Define the problem more precisely; **Making green rice for four people.**

### Problem Components:
Break down the problem into all its parts and sub-problems; **Rice, spinach, sausage, onion, oil, salt, and pepper.**

### Data Collection:
Research existing examples, compare, and discard duplicates; **Has anyone else done this before?**

### Data Analysis:
Identify the flaws in the collected examples; **How did they do it? What can I learn from them?**

### Creativity:
Think about how to assemble everything in the most creative and functional way; **How do I put everything together the right way?**

### Materials and Technologies:
Experiments with technologies and materials. Try to combine "known" elements in a different way; **Which rice? Which pot? Which heat source?**

### Experimentation:
Try using a technology in a different way than its original purpose.

### Verification:
Testing with a sample of users.

### Construction Drawings:
Clear and readable drawings in sufficient number to understand every detail.
