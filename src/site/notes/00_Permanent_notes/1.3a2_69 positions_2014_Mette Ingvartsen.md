## Conceptual and Bodily Framework

The work is situated within the research of **Expanded Choreography**, focusing on the displacement of movement agency. A fundamental premise is that the body is never "one." Ingvartsen is interested in the contemporary body, which she defines as decentralized, hyper-connected, hyper-stimulated, and sexualized. The goal is to create a **cross-historical body** that detaches itself from historical representation to become a heterochronic phenomenon, capable of mutating toward the future.

## Performance Methodology: Soft Choreography and Archives

- **Soft Choreography:** This concept is developed in contrast to "hard choreography." Soft choreography is an organizational mode of performance that does not rely on a sharp separation between performer and spectator, or between stage and stalls. It is a choreography that cannot exist without audience participation, as it is defined by the moods, relations, and tensions of the group at any given moment. It concerns the organization of space and collective behavior.
    
- **Performing Archives:** The performance _69 positions_ is conceived as an archive in action. It is not about a historiographical, faithful reconstruction of the past, but about using traces and archives of works from the 1960s and 70s (such as Carolee Schneemann’s _Meat Joy_ or _Dionysus in 69_) to reactualize them and make them palpable in the present.
    

## Choreography of Language and Participation

- **Language Choreography:** Language is used as a medium to create an immaterial "reality of words" and virtual relationships. Through narration, orality, and the description (_ekphrasis_) of performances, the text aims to produce a physical reality as intense and strong as the original event it describes.
    
- **Audience Role and Negotiation:** The audience is actively involved, called upon to potentially become an actor, a sculpture, or a participant. The required participation is often imaginary or intellectual rather than purely physical. A fundamental aspect is **audience hesitation** (the moment of uncertainty between acting or merely imagining), which is considered a crucial political moment.
    

## Sexuality, Politics, and Economy

- **Public and Private:** A central theme is the dissolution of boundaries between the public and private spheres. The objective is to extract the naked, sexual body from the private sphere and place it in the public space, demonstrating how sexuality participates in the construction of society and politics.
    
- **Pharmacopornographic Economy and Affect:** Ingvartsen explores Paul B. Preciado’s concept of the "**pornification of labor**," where sexuality, excitation, and desire are at the heart of political and economic production (_labor sexus est_). Any cultural production capable of stimulating cycles of excitement-frustration can be seen as pornographic (_porno-envy_). The work aims to counter affective manipulation and the capitalization of the sexual potential of bodies.
    
- **Sexual Self-Experimentation and Counter-Practices:** To resist control mechanisms and heteronormative norms, Ingvartsen proposes sexual self-experimentation and **contra-sexual practices**. These practices, also explored in _7 Pleasures_, include sexual interaction with objects (as in _Object Orgy_) or the use of non-human elements to produce pleasure (e.g., Testogel, electrostimulation). The purpose is to dismantle culturally and historically prescribed sexual behavior models (such as the passive/active divide). Nudity is no longer seen as a gesture of liberation (as in the 1960s), but as a tool for the **erasure** of identifying traits, allowing the body to transform and dissolve personal identity.
