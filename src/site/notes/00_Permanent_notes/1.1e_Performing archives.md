# Performing archives

A practice that allows for the past to be shown through an embodied approach. Therefore, it is not about trying to faithfully exhibit and analyze what happened in the past, but rather about showing it by ritualizing it. Performing Archives means making the past palpable in the present, through action and speculation.

> [[00_Permanent_notes/1.3a2_69 positions_2014_Mette Ingvartsen\|1.3a2_69 positions_2014_Mette Ingvartsen]]
>Ingvartsen's work on _69 Positions_ is dedicated to the notion of **Performing Archives**. Her primary objective is not to act as an art historian, attempting to faithfully exhibit and analyze what occurred in the past. Rather, it is about finding a way to demonstrate a performative understanding of the past, exposing the social and political conditions of a bygone era while simultaneously reactualizing the questions regarding social structures that were forcefully posed by the artistic practices of the 1960s.
