# 3.3a_How the Runa Puma define the world

The Runa Puma, an indigenous population of the Amazon, consider the surrounding world as possessing an "essence" with a self of its own. From trees to animals, they have a self. Only when they die do these become non-selves, objects. We can therefore say that death is becoming objects.

> [[00_VOULT/02_REFERENCE NOTES/How Forests Think_Eduardo Kohn_2021\|How Forests Think_Eduardo Kohn_2021]]
>"Being aware of the selfhood of the multiple beings that populate the cosmos poses particular challenges. The Runa enter the ecology of the forest's selves in order to hunt, which means they recognize the others as selves on a par with themselves, in order to transform them into non-selves."
